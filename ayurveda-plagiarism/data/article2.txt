In Ayurveda, arthritis is described as a condition resulting from the vitiation of Vata dosha along with the accumulation of Ama (toxins). The disease leads to joint inflammation, stiffness, and restricted movement. Ayurvedic treatment protocols emphasize the use of herbal medicines such as Boswellia serrata, Withania somnifera, and Commiphora mukul.
These herbs possess anti-inflammatory and immunomodulatory properties that help in reducing swelling and improving joint flexibility. Dietary management and avoidance of incompatible foods are also considered essential in preventing the progression of arthritis.
Therapeutic procedures such as Panchakarma are often prescribed to detoxify the body and restore doshic balance. Regular use of these treatments has shown promising results in improving the quality of life in arthritis patients.
