Ayurvedic management of arthritis primarily focuses on balancing the aggravated Vata dosha. According to classical Ayurvedic texts, joint disorders are associated with Vata imbalance leading to pain, stiffness, and reduced mobility. Herbal formulations such as Ashwagandha (Withania somnifera), Shallaki (Boswellia serrata), and Guggulu are widely used for their anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
Panchakarma therapies including Abhyanga (oil massage) and Basti (medicated enema) are recommended for detoxification and long-term management of arthritis. These therapies aim to remove accumulated toxins (Ama) and restore balance in the body.
Clinical observations suggest that combining herbal medicines with lifestyle modifications and dietary regulation significantly improves joint function and reduces chronic pain in patients suffering from osteoarthritis and rheumatoid arthritis.
