Traditional Ayurvedic therapies play a significant role in managing musculoskeletal disorders including arthritis. The concept of Ama and its role in disease manifestation is central to Ayurvedic pathology. Accumulation of toxins in the joints leads to inflammation and pain.
Treatment strategies include the administration of herbal formulations along with detoxification procedures such as Panchakarma. These approaches aim to eliminate toxins and rejuvenate the affected tissues.
Studies indicate that Ayurvedic interventions not only reduce joint pain but also enhance mobility and overall physical function. Integrating Ayurvedic principles with modern clinical practices may provide a holistic approach to arthritis management.
